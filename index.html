Week 1 — Foundations

Ascending Pause — Leg Press Drop Set — Rope Pushdown · Lying Leg Curl 1¼ Reps — Lat Pulldown AI — Machine Preacher Curl
Session A MON · ~60 MIN
Push Day
Warm-Up 5–8 min bike or treadmill before starting
Triceps — Pre-Fatigue
Overhead Cable Extension — Rope PRE-FATIGUE DROP SET
3 × 10 + drop
Drop Set on every set: Complete 10 reps, immediately reduce weight by 25–30%, continue for 6–8 more reps. No rest between the two parts. Done before chest to arrive at the press with triceps already working.
Cable machine, low pulley, rope attachment · Face away from the stack. Rope held overhead, elbows pointing to the ceiling — only your forearms move. Extend to full lockout. This loads the long head of the tricep in its fully stretched position. The drop set pushes beyond failure before the chest press even begins. Rest 75s.
Chest — Key Lift
Machine Chest Press KEY LIGHT
4 × 10
Hammer Strength or plate-loaded machine · Seat so handles sit at mid-chest. Tricep is pre-fatigued — the chest is carrying more load than the weight suggests. Don't chase numbers here. Push through the chest, exhale on the press. Rest 90s.
Triceps — Peak Contraction
Cable Pushdown — V-Handle
3 × 12
Cable machine, high pulley, V-handle · Elbows pinned at sides. Full extension at the bottom — squeeze hard. This works the tricep at its shortest, most contracted position — the opposite end of the strength curve to the overhead extension done before chest. Rest 60s.
Shoulders
Lateral Raise Machine LIGHT
3 × 15
Lateral raise machine · Arms to shoulder height only. Light weight, slow tempo. Side of the shoulder working — no shrugging, no swinging.
Abs
Cable Crunch — Kneeling CORE
3 × 15
Cable machine, high pulley, rope · Kneel facing the stack, rope behind your head. Crunch elbows toward knees — round the back fully. Hold the contracted position a moment. Exhale fully at the bottom. Control the return.
Ab Wheel Rollout CORE
3 × 8–10
From knees · Roll forward until torso is near parallel to the floor — only go as far as you can return from with control. Pull back by contracting abs and lats together. Reduce the distance until the range is there.
Session B WED · ~60 MIN
Pull Day
Warm-Up 5–8 min easy rowing before starting
Back — Key Lift
Lat Pulldown — Overhand Wide KEY 1¼ REPS
4 × 8
1¼ Rep Protocol: Pull the bar to your chest (full rep) · return it one quarter of the way up · pull back to chest again · then return to the top fully — that full sequence = 1 rep. The extra quarter rep at the fully contracted position doubles the time under tension at the point where the lat is working hardest. Challenging — reduce weight from your normal pulldown load.
Cable pulldown machine · Overhand grip, wider than shoulder-width. Slight lean back, pull to upper chest. The 1¼ protocol means every rep includes an additional peak contraction. Rest 90s.
Single-Arm Dumbbell Row
4 × 10 each
Bench support — hand and same-side knee on bench · Drive the dumbbell to your hip, not your shoulder. Back flat. All reps one side before switching. A weight you can control through the full range. Rest 75s.
Biceps — AI Method
Machine Preacher Curl AI
3 × 8
Ascending Pause at peak contraction: Rep 1 hold 1s at the top · Rep 2 hold 2s · Rep 3 hold 3s · continuing through to rep 8 (8s hold). The preacher machine keeps the arm in a fixed position — the ascending pause forces honest bicep contraction on every rep with nowhere to cheat. By rep 6 this will be genuinely demanding.
Machine preacher curl · Upper arm on the pad throughout — only the forearm moves. Curl to full flexion and hold for the prescribed seconds. Lower slowly — the eccentric is just as important as the contraction. Rest 90s.
Rear Delts
Face Pull
3 × 20
Cable machine, rope at eye height · Pull toward forehead, rotate wrists — thumbs finish behind ears. Light weight. Shoulder maintenance every session — non-negotiable.
Abs
Hanging Knee Raise CORE
3 × 12
Dead hang from pull-up bar · Tuck knees to chest — tilt the pelvis at the top, don't just lift the legs. Lower slowly and with control. Progress to straight-leg raises when this becomes comfortable.
Hollow Body Hold — Weighted CORE
3 × 30s
On back, arms overhead holding a light plate · Legs lifted, lower back pressed to the floor. The added load makes this significantly harder than a standard hold. Increase the weight rather than the time when 30s becomes easy.
Session C FRI · ~60 MIN
Legs Day
Warm-Up 5–8 min stationary bike before starting
Quads — Key Lift
Leg Press KEY PAUSE REPS
4 × 8
Ascending Pause at the bottom of each rep: Rep 1 hold 1s · Rep 2 hold 2s · Rep 3 hold 3s · through to rep 8 (8s hold). The pause at the bottom removes elastic rebound — every rep becomes a true concentric effort from a dead stop. This is significantly harder than standard leg press. Use a moderate weight, not your usual load. Rest 2 min.
Leg press machine · Feet shoulder-width, mid-platform. Lower to 90° and pause for the prescribed count. Drive through the full foot to press back. Don't lock out at the top.
Hamstrings
Romanian Deadlift
3 × 10
Barbell or dumbbells · Hinge at the hips, weight close to the body. Feel the hamstring stretch on the way down — when you feel it, drive the hips forward to return. Don't round the lower back. Moderate weight, full control. Rest 90s.
Lying Leg Curl DROP SET
3 × 10 + drop
Drop Set on final set only: Complete 10 reps, strip 25–30% immediately, continue for 6–8 more reps. No rest between the two parts. Together with the RDL, these two cover the hamstring at opposite ends of its strength curve — the RDL at the stretched position, the curl at peak contraction.
Lying leg curl machine · Pad just above heels. Curl toward backside and squeeze at the top. Slow return. Drop set on the last set only. Rest 90s.
Glutes
Bulgarian Split Squat
3 × 8 each leg
Rear foot elevated on bench, dumbbells at sides · Lower until front thigh is parallel — drive through the front heel to return. First set use a light dumbbell to find your footing, then load it. All reps one leg before switching. Rest 75s per leg.
Calves
Standing Calf Raise
4 × 15
Calf raise machine · Full range — deep stretch at the bottom, full rise at the top. Slow tempo, 2s up, 2s down. Pause at the stretch.
Abs
Landmine Rotation CORE
3 × 10 each side
Barbell in landmine attachment · Stand holding the end of the bar, arms slightly bent. Rotate in a controlled arc from hip height one side to the other. Trains rotational core strength — deliberate movement, not a swing.
Ab Wheel Rollout CORE
3 × 8
From knees · Same as Session A. Track whether range has improved. Pull back with abs and lats — not hip flexors.

Week 2 — Progression

Ascending Pause — Hack Squat Drop Set — EZ Bar Pushdown · Lying Leg Curl 1¼ Reps — Chest-Supported Row AI — Machine Preacher Curl
Session D MON · ~60 MIN
Push Day
Warm-Up 5–8 min bike or treadmill before starting
Triceps — Pre-Fatigue
Overhead Cable Extension — Rope PRE-FATIGUE
3 × 10
Same movement as Week 1, low pulley, rope overhead · Now you know the exercise well — focus on quality of the stretch at the bottom and full lockout at the top. Small weight increase if Week 1 felt controlled throughout. No drop set this week — saving that for the EZ bar later. Rest 60s.
Chest — Key Lift
Machine Chest Press KEY LIGHT
4 × 10
Same machine · Small weight increase only if Week 1 felt fully controlled at every rep. Tricep is pre-fatigued — chest doing the work. Keep load conservative. Rest 90s.
Triceps — Peak Contraction
EZ Bar Pushdown DROP SET
3 × 12 + drop
Drop Set on every set: 12 reps, immediately reduce weight by 25%, continue for 8 more reps. The EZ bar allows slightly more load than a rope or V-handle at this position — combined with the drop, this is a significant tricep finisher. Different attachment and drop protocol to Week 1's overhead extension drops.
Cable machine, high pulley, EZ bar · Grip the angled section overhand. Elbows pinned at sides, full extension at the bottom. Same peak contraction position as the V-handle in Week 1 but a different feel and load capacity. Rest 75s.
Shoulders
Cable Lateral Raise — Single Arm LIGHT
3 × 15 each
Cable machine, low pulley · Stand side-on, cable running across the body. Raise to shoulder height. The cable loads the delt from zero degrees unlike a machine or dumbbell — a more complete stimulus for the medial delt. Light weight. All 15 one side, then switch. Rest 45s.
Abs
Cable Crunch — Kneeling CORE
3 × 15
Same as Week 1 · Add a small amount of weight if last week felt manageable. Focus on the full exhale and the 1s hold at the contracted position on every rep.
Ab Wheel Rollout CORE
3 × 10
Up to 10 reps · If range has improved since Week 1, go further on each rollout. If fully extended from knees is controlled on all reps, progress to feet-elevated or standing rollout in the next block.
Session E WED · ~60 MIN
Pull Day
Warm-Up 5–8 min easy rowing before starting
Back — Key Lift
Chest-Supported Row Machine KEY 1¼ REPS
4 × 8
1¼ Rep Protocol: Pull to full retraction (full rep) · release one quarter of the way back · pull to full retraction again · then return fully to the start — that full sequence = 1 rep. The extra quarter rep at the fully contracted position doubles time under tension at peak scapular retraction. Reduce weight from your normal row load — this is harder than it sounds.
Hammer Strength chest-supported row · Chest on the pad, neutral grip handles. The 1¼ protocol paired with the chest support (no lower back cheating) makes this a very honest, demanding back exercise. A genuinely different stimulus to the lat pulldown 1¼ in Week 1. Rest 90s.
Straight-Arm Cable Pushdown
3 × 15
Cable machine, high pulley, rope · Arms nearly straight — soft bend only. Sweep the rope down toward your hips. Isolates the lat with zero bicep involvement. A finisher that feels completely unlike any row or pulldown. Rest 60s.
Biceps — AI Method
Machine Preacher Curl AI
3 × 8
Ascending Pause at peak contraction: Rep 1 hold 1s · Rep 2 hold 2s · Rep 3 hold 3s · through to rep 8 (8s hold). Same protocol as Week 1 — if weight felt manageable last week, add a small amount this session. The preacher pad removes any cheating — the ascending pause will be genuinely hard by rep 5 onward.
Machine preacher curl · Upper arm on the pad throughout. Curl to full flexion and hold. Lower slowly. Rest 90s.

Face Pull

Reverse Pec Deck
3 × 15
Pec deck machine, facing the pad · Arms open outward, lead with elbows. Squeeze the rear delts at the end point. Light weight — precision over load. A different rear delt stimulus to the face pull. Rest 60s.
Abs
Hanging Knee Raise — Weighted CORE
3 × 12
Dead hang, dumbbell held between feet · Tuck knees to chest — posterior pelvic tilt at the top. Lower with control. Even 2.5–5kg changes the demand significantly. Rest 60s.
Hollow Body Hold — Weighted CORE
3 × 30s
Slight weight increase from Week 1 if last session held position well · Lower back to the floor at all times. Quality of position over surviving the time.

Legs Day

Hack Squat Machine KEY PAUSE REPS

Ascending Pause at the bottom: Rep 1 hold 1s · Rep 2 hold 2s · through to rep 8 (8s hold). The hack squat places more demand on the VMO and quad sweep than the leg press — the ascending pause on top makes this the hardest lower body stimulus in the block. Choose a weight you can maintain depth on with good form. Rest 2 min.
Hack squat machine · Back against pad, feet slightly in front of hips. Lower to parallel — pause for the prescribed count — drive back up. A genuinely different quad stimulus to the Week 1 leg press.

3 × 15
Cable machine, low pulley, rope · Stand facing away from the stack, rope between your legs. Hinge at the hips — feel the stretch through glutes and hamstrings — then drive your hips forward to return. A hip hinge pattern under constant cable tension. Different stimulus and feel to the Week 1 RDL. Rest 75s.

3 × 10 + drop
Drop Set on final set only: 10 reps, strip 25–30%, continue for 6–8 more reps immediately. Same protocol as Week 1 — use a slightly heavier starting weight if Week 1 felt well controlled. Drop remains relative to the working weight. Rest 90s.
Lying leg curl machine · Squeeze at full flexion, slow return. Drop set on last set only.
Calves
Seated Calf Raise
4 × 20
Seated calf raise machine · The seated position bends the knee which takes the gastrocnemius out of the movement — this loads the soleus, a deeper calf muscle rarely trained directly. A completely different stimulus to the standing raise in Week 1. Full range, slow tempo.
Abs
Landmine Rotation CORE
3 × 10 each side

Coaching Notes

Tricep pre-fatigue — the reasoning. The overhead cable extension before the chest press means the tricep arrives at the press already partially fatigued. The chest picks up more of the load at a lighter total weight. This is intentional — it's a smarter way to train chest when total load needs to stay conservative.

Ascending pause reps — execution. Rep 1 hold 1 second at the contracted position. Rep 2 hold 2 seconds. Through to the final rep. The accumulating time under tension means a set of 8 becomes demanding by rep 5 or 6. Do not increase weight to compensate — the method is the load. Used on legs only, machine-based.

1¼ rep protocol — weight selection. Reduce weight by around 20–25% from your normal working load for that exercise. The extra quarter rep at the contracted position doubles time under tension at the point of peak muscle activation. It feels harder than it looks on paper — you'll find out on rep 4.

AI on the preacher curl. The machine keeps the upper arm fixed — there is nowhere to compensate. The ascending pause will feel manageable on rep 1 and genuinely hard by rep 6. If the weight used means you can't hold the prescribed seconds with the arm fully flexed, it's too heavy. Reduce and go again.

Drop sets — execution is everything. The drop must be immediate — no rest, no re-positioning. Have the next weight ready before you start the working set. The point is extending the set beyond where you'd normally stop. A sloppy drop set with rest in between is just two normal sets.

Strength curve pairing — triceps. The overhead extension loads the tricep in its longest, most stretched position. The V-handle or EZ bar pushdown loads it at its shortest, most contracted position. These are not two of the same exercise — they target the same muscle at opposite ends of its range. Both in the same session gives full-curve coverage.

Seated vs standing calf raise. The standing raise targets the gastrocnemius — the visible muscle. The seated raise in Week 2 targets the soleus underneath it, which is only accessible when the knee is bent. Training both gives a more complete calf stimulus than doubling up on the same machine.

Face pull — every session. Light weight, 20 reps, rope at eye height. Pull to forehead with external rotation at the end — thumbs behind the ears. Three minutes of work per session with significant cumulative benefit to shoulder health over weeks and months. It stays in the program long-term.
